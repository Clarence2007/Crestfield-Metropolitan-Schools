ADMISSIONS POLICY

Entrance Tests

Prospective Crestfield students are required to undergo a timed proctored test in the school premises. These tests are primarily just to evaluate the students academic abilities and see if they will be able to cope in the grade they plan on joining. Students are expected to score at or above grade-level in order to be granted admission.

Entrance Test Levels

Preschool Tests Pre Kindergarten, Lower Kindergarten and Upper Kindergarten

The Preschool entrance tests are designed to understand your child's developmental readiness. These assessments focus on basic skills such as recognizing shapes, colors, numbers, and letters. They also include activities that gauge fine motor skills and the ability to follow simple instructions. The goal is to ensure your child is prepared for the learning environment and can benefit from the preschool curriculum.

Primary School Tests Grade 1, Grade 2, Grade 3, Grade 4 and Grade 5

The Primary School entrance tests evaluate foundational skills in reading, writing, and mathematics. These assessments include exercises in basic literacy, number recognition, and simple problem-solving. The aim is to ensure your child is ready for the academic challenges of primary school and can thrive in a structured learning environment.

JSS1 Common Entrance

The JSS1 Common Entrance Tests are designed for Grade 5 students to assess their readiness for secondary school. These exams cover core subjects such as English, Mathematics, and General Knowledge, focusing on comprehension, analytical skills, and problem-solving abilities. The goal is to determine if students have the necessary knowledge and skills to transition smoothly into Junior Secondary School.

Secondary School Tests JSS2 momentarily...

The JSS2 entrance tests are designed to evaluate the academic progress of students who have completed their first year of secondary school. These assessments cover key subjects such as English, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies, with a focus on deeper comprehension, critical thinking, and application of knowledge. The goal is to ensure that students are well-prepared for the continued academic challenges of Junior Secondary School.





Age Policy

An age policy is essential in order to ensure that students who enter each grade are physically, mentally, socially and emotionally prepared. The Age Policy is effective from the beginning of the school year for new students.
